Knee Red Flags — Stop & Rest

Pain (not soreness) during squats, going downstairs, or lateral movements. Sharp pain behind the kneecap. Swelling. Any pain that changes your gait. These mean rest + ice + assess — not push through.

Run Types

Easy / Recovery: 6:00–8:30/km — conversational. This is 80% of your volume. Most people run these too fast.

Tempo: 5:50–6:10/km — comfortably hard. 20–40 min sustained.

Intervals: 5:15–5:35/km — hard efforts with full rest. 400m–1km repeats.

Long Run: 6:30–7:30/km — easy, build to 21–26km. Practice race nutrition.

Phase Overview

Phase 1 (Now–May 25): Base + speed work. Sub-2 prep.

Phase 2 (Jun–Jul): Trail adaptation, elevation, heat. Sinister 7 build.

Phase 3 (Jul–Aug): Peak race block. Death Race + Black Spur back-to-back. Manage carefully.

Phase 4 (Sep–Oct): Performance peak. Fort Mac PR attempt. Grizzly finale.

Off-season (Nov–Jan): Recomp. Lower volume, heavy strength.

Phase 5 (Feb–Mar): Big Island marathon build.

Weekly Structure

YOUR TRAINING WEEK

Monday: Easy run 6–8km + Strength A
Tuesday: Interval or Tempo run 8–12km
Wednesday: Strength B + optional easy 5km
Thursday: Easy-moderate run 8–12km
Friday: Strength C + strides (6×100m fast)
Saturday: Long run 15–26km (NO strength)
Sunday: Full rest or 30-min walk/yoga

Injury Prevention Running Rules

1. Never increase weekly mileage more than 10% week-over-week.
2. One quality session per week max in the 6 weeks before a race.
3. If your legs feel heavy after a long run — the next run is EASY. No exceptions.
4. Cadence target: 170–180 steps/min. Short quick steps protect knees.
5. Replace shoes every 600–700km. Midsole breakdown = knee pain.

Breakfast · ~400 cal

Protein Power Shake

Base: 200ml kefir + 1 scoop protein powder or 2 tbsp hemp seeds
Add: 1 cup mixed berries (frozen OK)
Seeds: 1 tbsp flaxseed + 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds + 1 tsp brewer's yeast

Simple Go-To Plate

Protein: 3 whole eggs (scrambled or hard boiled) + ½ cup cottage cheese
Carbs: 3–4 rice cakes
Fruit: 1–2 bananas
Optional: handful of walnuts or drizzle of almond butter

~38g protein · 55g carbs · 16g fat
Quick, portable, no cooking required. Perfect for busy mum life.

Dinner · ~550–650 cal

Protein + Starch + Veg

Protein (180–200g): Salmon · Chicken thigh · Lean beef · Turkey · Shrimp
Starch: 1–1.5 cups cooked rice OR 1 medium potato (any style)
Veg: 2 cups any roasted or steamed vegetables
Fat: Olive oil or butter for cooking

~42g protein · 55g carbs · 20g fat

Snacks · ~300–400 cal total

Strategic Snacking

Pre-run snack (if needed): 2 rice cakes + honey + banana — 30–60 min before

Post-run (within 30 min): Greek yogurt + berries + granola OR chocolate milk + protein shake

Evening: Greek yogurt (200g) + seeds — slow protein feeds overnight muscle repair + nursing

Post-run nutrition is your most important meal. Never skip it.

Race Week Fueling

RACE NUTRITION PROTOCOL

48hrs Before

Carb-load: 8–10g/kg bodyweight. Simple carbs. Pasta, rice, bread, banana. Cut fibre and fat. Salt your food.

Race Morning

Protein shake + 2 rice cakes + honey + banana. 90 min before gun. 500ml electrolytes. No new foods.

During Race

Gel every 40 min from km 5. Target 45–60g carbs/hour. Sip water at every station.

Hydration

4–5L daily on run days. Electrolytes (not just water) on all runs over 45 min. Breastfeeding raises sodium losses.

Post-Race

Protein shake within 20 min. Full meal within 90 min. Nurse or pump — refuel milk supply. Legs up + ice 20 min.

Supplements

Postnatal multi · Omega-3 DHA · Vit D3 · Magnesium glycinate · Tart cherry · Collagen + Vit C · Electrolytes daily
